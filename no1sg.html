My Reviewer's Guide

Build perfect sentences for your reviews!

1. Comparatives (A vs. B)

Use comparatives to compare two things.

Rule 1: Short Adjectives (+er)

For most short adjectives (1 syllable), add -er.

"The Terra backpack is heavier than the Aero."
"The Aero is cheaper than the new model."

Rule 2: Long Adjectives (more...)

For long adjectives (2+ syllables), use more.

"The Aero is more practical for school."
"The Terra is more durable."

Rule 3: Irregular Adjectives

These are special! You must memorize them.

  • Good → Better
  • Bad → Worse
"The Aero has better pockets for a laptop."

2. Superlatives (The Best)

Use superlatives to compare three or more things and find the #1.

Rule 1: Short Adjectives (+est)

For short adjectives, use the + -est.

"The Terra is the heaviest bag in the store."
"The Aero is the cheapest option."

Rule 2: Long Adjectives (most...)

For long adjectives, use the most.

"This is the most expensive backpack."
"It is the most comfortable for long trips."

Rule 3: Irregular Adjectives

  • Good → The Best
  • Bad → The Worst
"For city travel, the Aero is the best."

3. Contrast Connectors

Use these words to connect one good point (pro) and one bad point (con).

but (Simple Contrast)

Use but to join two simple ideas. Use a comma before it.

"The Terra is durable, but it is very heavy."

even though (Surprising Contrast)

Use even though to show a surprising contrast. It can start the sentence.

"Even though the Aero is expensive, I will buy it."
"I like the Aero, even though it costs more."

however (Formal Contrast)

Use however to separate two full sentences. It is more formal.

"The Terra is very durable. However, it is not waterproof."

4. Key Vocabulary

Tap each word to see an example sentence.

  • durable (strong, hard to break) +
    "The Terra backpack is very durable; it's made of strong canvas."
  • practical (useful, good for a situation) +
    "The Aero bag is more practical for students because it has a laptop pocket."
  • lightweight (not heavy) +
    "I prefer lightweight bags, so I chose the Aero (0.9 kg)."
  • heavy (not light) +
    "The Terra bag is heavy, even when it is empty."
  • waterproof (water cannot get inside) +
    "Living in a rainy city, a waterproof bag is the most practical."
  • expensive (costs a lot of money) +
    "The Aero is more expensive ($75) than the Terra ($45)."
  • cheap (costs little money) +
    "The Terra is the cheapest option, but it is not waterproof."
  • comfortable (feels good to use/wear) +
    "A good backpack must be comfortable if you walk all day."
