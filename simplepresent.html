Grammar Guide: The Simple Present Tense

What is the Simple Present?

The simple present is a verb tense we use to talk about conditions or actions happening right now or regularly. It is used for facts, habits, and routines. It is one of the most common and important tenses in English.

1. The Form (How to Build It)

Positive Sentences [+]

For he, she, and it, we add -s or -es to the end of the base verb.

SubjectVerb FormExample
I / You / We / They Base Verb (work, study) They work at a bank.
He / She / It Base Verb + s/es (works, studies) She works at a bank.

Negative Sentences [-]

We use the auxiliary verbs do not (don't) or does not (doesn't) + the base verb. Never add -s to the main verb in a negative sentence.

SubjectVerb FormExample
I / You / We / They do not (don't) + Base Verb They don't work at a bank.
He / She / It does not (doesn't) + Base Verb She doesn't work at a bank.

Questions [?]

We start the question with Do or Does, followed by the subject and the base verb.

Auxiliary VerbSubjectExample
Do you / we / they Do they work at a bank?
Does he / she / it Does she work at a bank?

2. The Uses (When to Use It)

Habits and Routines

Actions that happen regularly.

I drink coffee every morning.

He goes to the gym on Mondays and Wednesdays.

Permanent Truths and Facts

Things that are always true.

The Earth revolves around the Sun.

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

Schedules and Timetables

Future events that are part of a fixed schedule.

Our flight leaves at 9:00 AM tomorrow.

The meeting starts in ten minutes.

3. Subject and Object Pronouns

Pronouns replace nouns. Subject pronouns do the action. Object pronouns receive the action.

Subject PronounObject PronounExample
ImeI like coffee. The barista knows me.
YouyouYou are a student. I teach you.
HehimHe is my brother. I live with him.
SheherShe is the manager. I report to her.
ItitIt is a good book. I am reading it.
WeusWe are a team. The coach trains us.
TheythemThey are my clients. I help them.
