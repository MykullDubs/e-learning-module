Grammar Guide: Articles

(a, an, the)

What Are Articles?

Articles are small but very important words that come before a noun. They tell us whether we are talking about a general thing or a specific thing. The two types of articles are indefinite (a, an) and definite (the).

1. The Indefinite Article (a / an)

Use the indefinite article for a singular, countable noun that is general, not specific, or mentioned for the first time.

I need a phone. (Any phone, not a specific one)

She is an engineer. (Her profession is one of many engineers)

I saw a dog in the park. (The first time I mention this dog)

The Rule: A vs. An

The choice between a and an depends on the sound at the start of the next word, not the letter.

ArticleUse Before...Examples
a words starting with a consonant sound. a book, a car, a university (starts with "y" sound)
an words starting with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u). an apple, an idea, an umbrella, an hour (h is silent)

2. The Definite Article (the)

Use the for a noun that is specific. This means both the speaker and the listener know which one is being discussed.

When a noun is mentioned again:

I saw a dog in the park. The dog was chasing a ball.

When there is only one of something:

The sun is very bright today.

Who is the CEO of this company?

With superlative adjectives:

This is the best coffee in Cuernavaca.

It was the most interesting lesson.

3. The Zero Article (No Article)

Sometimes, we use no article at all. This is called the "zero article."

With plural or uncountable nouns in a general sense:

— Dogs are friendly animals. (Dogs in general)

The dogs in my neighborhood are loud. (Specific dogs)

— Education is important. (Education in general)

The education I received was excellent. (Specific education)

With most proper nouns:

We are flying to — Mexico City.

— Maria is my colleague.
