A1 Health & Medicine

Talking About Health

Sometimes we feel great, and sometimes we feel sick. It is very important to know how to tell someone how you are feeling. In this lesson, we will learn the basic words to talk about your health.

Health Vocabulary

a headache

Flip Card ↻
  • I **have** *a headache*.
  • My head hurts. I think I **have** *a headache*.
  • He **has** *a bad headache* today.
  • She is taking medicine for her *headache*.

feel better

Flip Card ↻
  • I was sick, but now I **feel better**.
  • You should drink water to **feel better**.
  • He took medicine and now he **feels better**.
  • I hope you **feel better** soon!

Grammar Focus 🩺

'To feel' + Adjective

Use the verb **feel** to describe your state or condition.

  • I **feel good**.
  • She **feels sick**.
  • They **feel tired**.

'To have' + Noun (Ailment)

Use the verb **have** to say what problem you have.

  • I **have a** headache.
  • He **has a** sore throat.
  • We **have** colds.

Common Conversation

Q: How are you feeling?
  • (+ Positive) I feel great, thanks!
  • (+ Positive) Much better, thank you.
  • (- Negative) Not so great.
  • (- Negative) Not well. I have a headache.
