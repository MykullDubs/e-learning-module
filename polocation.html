Grammar Guide: Prepositions of Location

What are Prepositions of Location?

Prepositions of location are words that answer the question "Where?". They describe the position of a person or thing in relation to another person or thing. Mastering the "Big Three"—in, on, and at—is the most important first step.

1. The "Big Three": IN, ON, AT

Think of these prepositions in terms of dimensions: in is for enclosed spaces (3D), on is for surfaces (2D), and at is for specific points (1D).

2. Prepositions of Relative Position

These words describe where something is in relation to something else.

Above / Over: The lamp is above the table.

Below / Under: The cat is sleeping under the chair.

Beside / Next to: He sits next to me in class.

Between: The pharmacy is between the bank and the supermarket.

Among: I found the letter among a pile of old papers.

In front of: The car is parked in front of the house.

Behind: The garden is behind the house.

Near / Close to: We live near a park.

3. Prepositions of Movement

These words describe the path of movement from one place to another.

To: I am going to the store.

From: She came from Japan.

Into / Out of: He walked into the room and then walked out of it.

Onto / Off of: The cat jumped onto the roof and then jumped off of it.

Across: We swam across the river.

Through: The train goes through a tunnel.

4. Common Mistakes

CorrectExplanation
He is in the car.Use in for small, private vehicles.
She is on the bus.Use on for large, public transport where you can walk.
I am at home.At is used for general locations like home, work, or school.
The picture is on the wall.A picture hangs on the surface of the wall.
