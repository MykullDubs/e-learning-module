From Action to Strategy

At the B2 level, it's not enough to say you exercise; you must be able to discuss your strategy, analyze your progress, and consider hypothetical changes to your routine. This lesson provides the language to turn your fitness plan into a sophisticated conversation.

Strategic Fitness Vocabulary

hit a plateau

Flip Card ‚Üª
  • If I **hit a plateau**, I will change my workout routine.
  • It's common for runners to **hit a plateau** after a few months of training.
  • She **hit a plateau** with her weight loss, so she consulted a nutritionist.
  • What would you do if you **hit a plateau**?

quantify progress

Flip Card ‚Üª
  • You need to **quantify progress** to know if your plan is effective.
  • I **quantify progress** by tracking my run times and the weights I lift.
  • If she doesn't **quantify progress**, she won't stay motivated.
  • Let's **quantify progress** by measuring it every two weeks.

Quantifying Goals & Progress

Use prepositions and specific phrases to make your goals measurable.

  • (Improve **by** a value) "I want to improve my kilometer time **by** one minute."
  • (Get **down to** a target) "...and get it **down to** 6:30."
  • (Increase **by** a value) "My goal is to increase my bench press **by** 10kg."
  • (Cut **down on** something) "I need to cut **down on** sugary drinks."

First & Second Conditionals

Discuss real possibilities and hypothetical situations in your fitness plan.

First Conditional (Real Future Possibility)

If + Present Simple, ... will + Verb

If I **stick** to my diet, I **will lose** 5kg.

Second Conditional (Hypothetical/Unreal)

If + Past Simple, ... would + Verb

If I **had** a home gym, I **would train** every day.
