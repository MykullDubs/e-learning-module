IELTS Vocabulary Tiers

A guide to upgrading your everyday words for a higher score.

Instead of: Good

Try using:

  • Exceptional
  • Remarkable
  • Captivating
  • Fascinating

"The museum had a fascinating collection of ancient artifacts."

Instead of: Bad

Try using:

  • Detrimental
  • Adverse
  • Harmful
  • Disastrous

"Heavy pollution has a detrimental effect on the local ecosystem."

Instead of: Important

Try using:

  • Crucial
  • Vital
  • Pivotal
  • Indispensable

"Access to clean water is vital for the health of the community."

Instead of: Interesting

Try using:

  • Compelling
  • Intriguing
  • Thought-provoking
  • Engaging

"The documentary presented a compelling argument for renewable energy."

Instead of: Big

Try using:

  • Substantial
  • Considerable
  • Significant
  • Vast

"The government has made a substantial investment in public transport."

Instead of: Happy

Try using:

  • Delighted
  • Thrilled
  • Overjoyed
  • On cloud nine (Idiom)

"I was on cloud nine when I heard I had received the scholarship."

Instead of: Show

Try using:

  • Illustrates
  • Depicts
  • Demonstrates
  • Compares

"The bar chart illustrates the different methods of transport used by commuters in London."

Instead of: Use

Try using:

  • Utilize
  • Employ
  • Leverage
  • Adopt

"Modern businesses should leverage social media to reach a wider audience."

Instead of: New

Try using:

  • Innovative
  • Contemporary
  • Cutting-edge
  • Novel

"The company is known for its innovative approach to solving complex problems."
