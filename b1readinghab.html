Giving a Book Recommendation

CEFR Level: B1

Use the passive voice and present perfect continuous to share your opinions.

Time to Share!

You know how to talk about your reading habits. Now, let's learn how to give a recommendation! In this lesson, you will learn the language to describe books and share what you've been reading with friends.

Vocabulary for Reviews

Click a card to learn words for describing a book.

plot

The story or events of a book.
"The plot was very exciting."

character

A person in a story.
"My favorite character was the hero."

setting

The time and place of a story.
"The setting is a fantasy world."

recommend

To suggest something is good.
"I recommend this book."

Present Perfect Continuous

Use this for actions that started in the recent past and are still happening (or just finished). It's perfect for talking about what you're reading "these days".

"What have you been reading recently?"

"I've been reading a fantasy series from an American author."

Grammar Transformer: Active vs. Passive Voice

We use the passive voice when the action is more important than who did it. Click the button to see the sentence transform!

J.R.R. Tolkien wrote The Hobbit in 1937.

The Hobbit was written in 1937.
